Kuwait Implements Global Minimum Tax

Law No. 157 of 2024 & Executive Regulations – Ministerial Resolution No. 55 of 2025

Tax Policy • 8 min read

Executive Summary

In line with the OECD's Pillar Two framework, Kuwait has formally enacted a comprehensive Global Minimum Tax regime to ensure multinational enterprises meet minimum tax obligations.

Law Enacted: Law No. 157 of 2024 (signed December 26, 2024)
Published: Official Gazette No. 1672 (December 30, 2024)
Regulations: Ministerial Resolution No. 55 of 2025
Published: Official Gazette No. 1685 (June 9, 2025)
Effective Date: Fiscal years beginning on or after January 1, 2025

Who Is Covered?

Multinational Enterprise Groups

The law applies to Multinational Enterprise Groups (MNEs) that meet specific revenue and presence criteria:

business
Kuwait Presence: Having one or more Constituent Entities in Kuwait
attach_money
Revenue Threshold: Consolidated revenue ≥ EUR 750 million
analytics
Historical Requirement: Revenue threshold met in at least 2 of the 4 prior fiscal years

Key Compliance Requirements

Kuwait Constituent Entities

In-scope Kuwait Constituent Entities must fulfill the following obligations:

1
Register with the Kuwait Tax Authorities
2
File a GloBE Information Return annually
3
Provide all information and documentation as per prescribed forms
4
Maintain financial and tax data relevant for GloBE calculation

Top-Up Tax Framework

When Top-Up Tax Applies

If jurisdictional Effective Tax Rate (ETR) < 15%, a Top-Up Tax applies

Tax Calculation Formula

ETR =
Covered Taxes
GloBE Income
Top-Up Tax = 15% – ETR

Key Definitions

Covered Taxes
"Taxes on income or profits, including taxes on distributed profits, and any other taxes as determined by the Minister."
GloBE Income or Loss
"The income or loss determined for each Constituent Entity in accordance with the GloBE Rules, before any elimination of intra-group transactions or consolidation adjustments, and after making the necessary adjustments as prescribed under the GloBE Rules and the Minister's decisions."

Filing Requirements

person

Who Files?

The following entities may file the GloBE Information Return:

  • The Ultimate Parent Entity (UPE)
  • A Designated Filing Entity under qualified exchange agreements
description

What Is Filed?

GloBE Information Return must include:

  • Identity and role of each Constituent Entity
  • GloBE Income, Covered Taxes, ETR details
  • Jurisdictional Top-Up Tax calculation
  • Other disclosures and reconciliations (as prescribed)
