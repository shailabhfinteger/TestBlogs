Executive Summary

The Federal Tax Authority (FTA) has issued Public Clarification CTP006 to clarify conditions for the AED 10,000 penalty waiver for late Corporate Tax registration, providing relief to qualifying taxpayers.

Original Penalty: AED 10,000 for late Corporate Tax registration
Legal Basis: Cabinet Decision No. 75 of 2023
Waiver Approved: UAE Cabinet approval in April 2025
Clarification Issued: CTP006 on July 2, 2025
Key Requirement: File tax return within 7 months from end of first tax period

The 7-Month Threshold

Filing Deadline for Penalty Relief

To qualify for waiver or refund, the tax return must be filed within 7 months from the end of:

Important Note

Reduced Timeline
This effectively shortens the filing timeline from 9 months to 7 months - for penalty relief purposes only.

Practical Impact

Key Implications

  • Filing after 7 months but before 9 months still avoids late filing penalties, but not the registration penalty
  • Burden shifts to the taxpayer to align filing schedules with relief timelines
  • Applies even where registration has been regularized

Exempt Entities

  • Filing requirement applies regardless of when exemption is granted
  • Late registrants still need to comply with the 7-month deadline for relief
  • Must file CT declaration within the specified timeline

Previous Requests

  • Reconsideration requests already granted prior to this clarification
  • Do not make taxpayers eligible for a second refund or credit
  • One-time relief mechanism

Registered Late + Penalty Paid + Filed Within 7 Months

Scenario 3

Registered Late + Obtained Exemption + Filed Within 7 Months

Conditions: Obtained exemption status, CT declaration filed within 7 months

Result: WAIVER / CREDIT / REFUND

Scenario 4

Filed After 7 Months But Within 9 Months

Conditions: Registered late, filed CT declaration after 7 months but within 9 months

Result: NOT ELIGIBLE FOR WAIVER / CREDIT / REFUND

Tax Groups

groups

Group Filing Requirements

If the group CT return is filed within 7 months from the end of the Group's first tax period, the AED 10,000 registration penalty imposed on each member for their individual late registration may be waived.

Condition: Group return must cover the first tax period of each member.

priority_high

Key Considerations

  • Group-Based Relief: Relief flows from the group return, not individual returns
  • Group Timeline: 7-month deadline based on group's first tax period
  • Coverage Requirement: Member's first tax period must be covered in group return
  • Refund Destination: Credits go to Tax Group account, not individual entities
